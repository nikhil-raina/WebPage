Past

I am from Bangalore, India. Well, originally from the North of India, to be specific: Delhi. However, I shifted
from Bangalore from 2nd grade onwards and had been there till I had to leave for University at the age of 18 years.
While I was in Bangalore 2 schools, Greenwood High (ICSE) and Canadian International School Bangalore (IB).

As far as I remember, I loved to fool around, play around, laugh about with friends and absolutely loved building Lego.

The very concept of building and creating my own “Master Piece”, in a way at least, was far too entertaining for
me to let go. Plus, at that age, children basically needed some good way to kill their time. I would usually do
it by playing cricket, swimming, badminton, eating or just building things with Lego like: monuments, guns, robots,
cars, bikes and buildings. As time went by, my mother and her sisters started to realize my passion and enjoyment
towards “playing”, that’s what they would always say… such novice individuals, with Lego, I started to get more
and more Lego puzzles. Each increasing in complexity. Eventually I was introduced to Mind Storm, which was a
GAME CHANGER!

Having been able to literally program your robot and make it move or sense or hold objects, was mind blowing to me!

I would always first follow the instructions to get used to building them, but eventually one main attribute stuck
with me till date, from all the different kinds of Legos I came across. The need to break and recreate something
better, at least that’s what I thought I was trying to do.

After making some robot of mine, I would take a picture of it and keep it aside, as a “momentary” satisfaction
that I was able to build it by myself. Since I was getting many Lego puzzles, I had a decent abundance for the
pieces, however after about 3 to 4 days, instead of looking at the new Lego puzzles, I would go back to the
previous model and lose my satisfaction from it. So, I would reduce it a bit and try to modify it or just dismantle
it completely and build it from scratch.

I believe that this was the introduction of my creativity and looking at things from a different perspective.
This learning or realization started to get implemented, innately, in many other day to day chores and studies.

Present

Currently, I am a student pursuing my bachelor’s degree in Computer Science at RIT, NY. I am in my 3rd year now
and actively looking around for Summer, Fall and Spring Internships.

So far, I have gotten into a few hackathons and 2 unpaid internships. Each have been really challenging for
me as the things that I learnt from there were new and helpful for my later assignments. Something like a ripple effect.
Learning work methods from courses, new languages from hackathons and new API implementations and use of libraries
from internships each have helped me from one assignment to another.

Through this variety of experience, I was able to explore my curiosity in the fields that I worked on.
